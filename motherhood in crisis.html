Reading 3:

Looking back on the articles I read in "Motherhood in Crisis," I find each one of them to be incredible moving stories that some people would be able to relate to at some level even if in different ways. The way in which the stories are told are also creative and interesting. Using a comic book design to introduce readers to the people and setting is a wonderful way to capture someone's attention. Long articles filled with words and few pictures can be easy to skim over if not outright ignore or grow bored of; but comic designs often capture people's attention easily with their coloring and short amount of reading. The concept of showing the story instead of only telling it through words is a topic I often seen debated and discussed online. Some people would rather see what's happening than read about it, which in turn would usually get them to care more. The color of the images also gives the article more life than a plain white or black background could. Adding a real video towards the end was a good way to remind people that these stories are real and so are the people involved in these stories. The fact that each story was interactive and allowed the readers to be able to interact with the screen and story and the way many people would otherwise not be able too is very creative as well. It allow people to take apart as if they were in the stories themselves as another good way for them to keep readers locked in to the stories being told to us.
