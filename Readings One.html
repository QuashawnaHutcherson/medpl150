I chose this image because it was a beautiful day and I thought that Central Park while covered in snow looked really beautiful coupled with the clear blue sky. It's not often I get to travel by Central Park outside of classes and appointments, so I thought it would be a good opportunity to do so while I was downtown in the area. When taking this photo I wanted the sky, namely the area where the sun was shinning and the area void of it's brightness to stand out while also capturing the trail left by a passing plane. This is because I wanted to add what I felt was a bit of an imaginative layer to it, as the way a certain area of the stream trail glowed in the sunlight made it feel like rather than it being from a plane, it felt like it was part of the sunlight or a bright ring as well. While the way that the tree branches looked without leaves reminded me of a rare discharged I learned in science called Ground to sky lighting, given the way the branches would fork and spread outward.
